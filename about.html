Research

Path-embedded Structural Human Brain Network Generation And Classification On Alzheimer’s Disease

Published on "Human Brain Mapping 2018"
Zhou Zhuang, Yan Jin, Hanghang Tong, Lei Shi, Yang Chen, Paul M. Thompson

The study of the human connectome can be beneficial for understanding how the brain changes in Alzheimer’s disease and normal aging. Most existing network generation practices build each subject’s structural brain connectivity network as an end-to-end network model. In these approaches, topological information on fiber tract paths is bypassed. Here we propose a new, path-embedded treatment of the fiber tracts, in which the intermediate ROIs, that each fiber goes through, are taken into account, in addition to the starting and ending ROIs.

Visual Analysis of Collective Anomalies Through High-Order Correlation Graph

Published on "IEEE Pacific Visualization Symposium 2018"; [PDF|Demo|Video]
Jun Tao, Lei Shi, Zhou Zhuang, Congcong Huang, Purui Su, Chaoli Wang, Yang Chen

High-Order Correlation Graph is a visualization tool designed for detecting, analyzing and reasoning collective anomalies for complex systems. We design elaborate visual metaphors, interaction models, and the coordinated multiple view based interface to allow users to fully unleash the visual analytics power over HOCG.

DeepScan: Exploiting Deep Learning for Malicious Account Detection in Location-Based Social Networks

Published on "IEEE Communications Magazine 2018"; [PDF]
Qingyuan Gong, Yang Chen, Xinlei He, Zhou Zhuang, Tianyi Wang, Hong Huang, Xin Wang, Xiaoming Fu

"DeepScan" is a malicious account detection system designed for local based social networks. DeepScan leverages emerging deep learning technologies to learn users’ dynamic behavior. It combines time series features and a set of convolutional features extracted from user activities, and exploits a Bi-LSTM for learning and detection.

Which Platform is More Favorable? A Cross-Platform Consumer Behavior Analysis of Large-Scale Mobile Shopping Data

Published on "International World Wide Web Conference 2018"; [PDF]
Hong Huang, Bo Zhao, Hao Zhao, Zhou Zhuang, Xiaoming Yao, Xinggang Wang, Ramin Yahyapour, Hai Jin, Xiaoming Fu

In this paper, we examine the consumer behaviors across multiple platforms based on a largescale mobile Internet dataset from a major telecom operator, which covers 9.8 million users from two regions among which 1.4 million users have visited e-commerce platforms within one week of our study.
